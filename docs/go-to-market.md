# GeoPulse Go-To-Market

## Positioning

GeoPulse should be sold as:

> Understand what happened, why markets care, and what to watch next.

That is intentionally narrower than a generic global-news product. The strongest first-year audience is finance-curious retail investors, macro newsletter readers, and self-directed ETF or sector traders who want geopolitical context without reading ten separate articles.

## Why This Wedge

- SmartNews reported in April 2025 that 71% of Americans consume little to no foreign news, but Millennials and Gen Z consume more than older cohorts, and economic issues and military conflicts were the top foreign-news topics of interest at 51% each.
- The same SmartNews study found social media is the most-used foreign-news source at 35%, but only 19% trust it. That trust gap is an opening for a product that explains source count, freshness, and cross-source support.
- Reuters Institute’s 2024 Digital News Report reported that only 17% of people across richer markets paid for any online news in the last year, so GeoPulse should not expect mass-market news-subscription behavior.
- Pew reported in 2024 that 85% of Americans had not paid for local news in the last year, and nearly half of non-payers cited free alternatives as the main reason.

Implication:

- GeoPulse needs to earn payment through investor utility, not through generic access to headlines.
- The anonymous preview must prove trust and usefulness quickly.
- The free plan must be strong enough to activate serious users.
- Premium should sell capacity, speed, personalization, and habit loops.

## Pricing Context

GeoPulse is currently positioned at `$8/month` or `$79/year`.

That sits below several adjacent information products:

- Ground News Premium is listed at `$39.99/year`, with Vantage at `$99.99/year`.
- TradingView Essential is listed at `$12.95/month` billed annually, with higher tiers at `$29.95`, `$59.95`, and above.
- Koyfin lists a free tier, Plus at `$39/month`, and Premium at `$79/month`.

Interpretation:

- GeoPulse should not price like a full terminal or charting workstation.
- The current Premium price is credible for a consumer-facing investor intelligence workflow if the product earns daily habit and trust.
- The free tier should stay genuinely useful, because many potential buyers are trained by adjacent products to start free and upgrade only after habit forms.

## Conversion Ladder

The product flow in the app now follows this sequence:

1. Anonymous preview
2. Free account
3. Premium subscription

### Anonymous Preview

The homepage now exposes a live public preview:

- top high-signal stories
- why-it-matters explanations
- regional hotspots
- delayed market snapshots

The preview is meant to answer three questions fast:

- Is the signal quality high enough?
- Does this help me understand market exposure?
- Is this worth creating an account for?

### Free Account

Free accounts should remain strong:

- full dashboard access
- timeline and drill-down pages
- daily morning brief
- 1 watchlist
- 3 alerts
- 3 saved views
- 5 digest stories

This is the activation layer. It should be good enough for real use.

### Premium

Premium should sell daily-operator convenience, not basic access:

- unlimited alerts
- unlimited watchlists
- unlimited saved views
- deeper explainers and premium insights
- faster market refresh when provider-backed access is enabled
- richer briefing workflows, including intraday delivery

## 1,000-Customer Math

OpenView’s product-led benchmarks report that the average freemium product gets about 6% sign-up conversion from website visitors and about 5% free-to-paid conversion from sign-ups.

If GeoPulse performed only at those averages, getting to 1,000 paid customers would require roughly:

- about 333,000 site visitors
- about 20,000 sign-ups
- about 1,000 paid users

That is possible, but too reliant on broad top-of-funnel traffic for a niche product.

A better first-year plan is to target higher-intent traffic and outperform generic freemium benchmarks:

- 40,000 to 60,000 qualified preview visitors
- 6,000 to 9,000 free accounts
- 1,000 paid users from the most activated segment

That requires:

- a strong preview-to-signup CTA
- excellent first-session activation
- a habit loop around the morning brief
- premium upsell tied to real workflow limits, not vague promises

## Likely Early Customers

The best first-year customers are likely to be:

- self-directed ETF and sector investors tracking oil, gold, defense, China, or rates
- macro newsletter readers who want a faster, more structured daily operating surface
- younger, digitally native investors comfortable with AI-assisted research workflows
- users who distrust social/news feeds but still want fast market-context summaries

These users are more likely to respond to:

- “what moved and why”
- morning brief workflows
- saved regional or sector views
- trust signals such as source count, freshness, and duplicate clustering

## Product Priorities For This Goal

To reach 1,000 paying customers, the roadmap should keep emphasizing:

- trust and relevance over volume
- daily habit formation over novelty
- clear free-vs-premium differences
- explainers and saved workflows over raw feeds
- acquisition surfaces that show product value without forcing signup first
